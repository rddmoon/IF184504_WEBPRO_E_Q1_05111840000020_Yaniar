Gresik Local Foods


This the list of my top 5 local foods in my hometown.



1. Nasi Krawu


One of the local foods of Gresik City that is very well known. Nasi krawu is one of the most popular culinary delights among the Gresik people. Its is usually served on a banana leaf. The warm fluffier rice served with stewed beef, sliced beef, beef offal and chili paste. This dish appearance is indeed simple but the taste and aroma is very tempting.

What's the difference between ordinary nasi krawu and Gresik nasi krawu? Of course it's different because Gresik nasi krawu is made from a special recipe. Nasi krawu consists of rice, chili paste, shredded meat (be it chicken or beef, but mostly beef), serundeng, and belacan. You can also add side dishes such as tofu, eel, tripe, intestines, fried lungs, and beef intestine stew. Gresik nasi krawu can be considered more special because it has more menu options.

2. Sego Roomo


Sego roomo is a local food from the City of Gresik which at first glance looks like an orange marrow porridge. However, the orange color of the sego roomo is a thick, savory and delicious sauce made from chilies and roasted prawns that are grounded until smooth.

Sego roomo is served uniquely. White rice is served with "pincuk" banana leaves mixed with cassava leaves, peanut brittle or crackers. Then, thick sauce is poured on top of it and added with a sprinkling of serundeng. The taste, of course, is very delicious.

If you visit Gresik, it will be very easy for you to find this food. There are many street vendors selling sego roomo. Sego roomo can also be found in traditional markets in the city of Gresik.

3. Otak-otak Bandeng


Otak-otak bandeng is a local food of Gresik City which is made from bandeng (milkfish). The fish is processed by separating the meat and skin, then mashed. Once smooth, the milkfish is flavored with a unique Gresik recipe and put back into the fish's body. After that, the milkfish is steamed until cooked.

Otak-otak bandeng is one of the top most favorite culinary delights in Gresik. So, if you travel to Gresik, don't forget to try this food.

4. Pudak


Pudak is one of the local foods of Gresik City that is no less delicious. Pudak is made from a mixture of coconut milk, brown sugar and rice flour. The ingredients are then mixed until even, then wrapped in banana leaves. Lastly, the wrapped mixture is steamed until cooked.

Pudak is also called ope cake. There is a type of pudak that is made from sago, it is called sago pudak. The white-colored pudak uses sugar to make it taste sweeter. Meanwhile, if the pudah is red-colored, it means that the pudak uses brown sugar.

5. Jubung


Jubung is a local Gresik food made from black sticky rice. The top of the Jubung is sprinkled with sesame seeds. Jubung is wrapped using the bark of the areca tree, so the appearance of Jubung looks very unique. The bark of the areca tree is called by local residents as "jubung".

Jubung is easy to find. There are so many sellers that can be found along the streets in Gresik.
