My Hometown


A description about my hometown.

Gresik, East Java, Indonesia.


Gresik is a regency in East Java province. It is bordered by Surabaya and the Madura Strait to the east, Lamongan to the west, the Java Sea to the north, Sidoarjo and Mojokerto to the south. Gresik also includes Bawean Island, which is 150 km off the Java Sea.

Gresik is known as the city where the first and largest cement company in Indonesia was founded, namely Semen Gresik. The name Gresik came from the word giri gisik, which means mountain on the shore, referring to the topography of Gresik which is located on the shore. The Gresik Government Center is located in Gresik District.

Most of the Gresik area is a coastal area, which extends from Kebomas, Gresik, Manyar, Bungah, Sidayu, Ujung Pangkah and Panceng Districts, as well as Sangkapura and Tambak Districts, which are located on Bawean Island. Most of the soil types in Gresik are relatively barren limestone.

Gresik is famous as the City of Walisongo, this is marked by historical exploration of the role and existence of the Walis whose graves are in Gresik, namely, Sunan Giri and Syekh Maulana Malik Ibrahim.
